CropSight
Aerial Crop Health Intelligence Platform
Build an AI-powered dashboard that ingests drone or satellite aerial images of farmland, uses
computer vision to detect crop stress and irrigation anomalies, and generates an actionable
geo-tagged health map a farmer can act on within the hour.

THE CHALLENGE
Smallholder farmers managing 2–20 hectare plots often cannot detect early-stage crop stress until visible yield loss
has already occurred. Manual field scouting is time-intensive and misses the subtle spectral changes that precede
visible wilting. Your task is to close this gap.
What you're given
RGB or multispectral aerial images (JPEG / PNG /
GeoTIFF) captured at 10–50 m altitude, covering one
or more farm plots. Images may include GPS EXIF
metadata for geo-referencing.
What you need to build
An overlay map colour-coded by crop health zone, a
severity score per zone, a ranked action list for the
farmer, and an exportable PDF or JSON report.
WHAT A COMPLETE SUBMISSION LOOKS LIKE
A working image pipeline
Your system should accept an aerial farm image as
input and produce a zoned health map as output —
without manual intervention beyond uploading the file.
A farmer-readable map
Zones should be colour-coded and labelled clearly
enough that someone without a technical background
can understand where to act and why.
A severity report
Each detected zone should carry a health score or
severity indicator, along with a recommended action
— even if it's just a simple text description.
A live demo
You should be able to upload a fresh image during
your 3-minute presentation and show the pipeline
running end to end in real time.
SUGGESTED 24-HOUR TIMELINE

This is a guide, not a rule. Adjust it to your team's strengths — but make sure you leave enough time at the end to polish your demo
and practice your pitch.
T + 0 h
Kickoff & Dataset Setup
Download and inspect your chosen dataset. Define team roles: model, backend, frontend. Confirm your tech stack.
T + 3 h
Image Pre-processing
Get your image pipeline working — loading, tiling, and colour conversion. Validate on a handful of sample images
before moving on.
T + 8 h
Core AI / Detection
Build and test your first working predictions on the farm images. Don't aim for perfection — aim for something
running.
T + 15 h
Dashboard & Map
Build the farmer-facing interface: image upload, colour-coded health map, per-zone detail panel. Wire your model
output to the UI.
T + 20 h
Polish & Report
Add your report export, tidy edge cases, and do a full internal run-through. Leave at least 4 hours for this — it always
takes longer.
T + 24 h
Submission
3-minute pitch: problem → what you built → live demo → real-world impact. Keep it concise and let the demo speak.
GROUND RULES
Work independently
Each team's solution must be their own. Pre-trained
models and open datasets are encouraged —
copying another team's code is not.
Depth over breadth
A polished, limited solution that does one thing well
will score higher than an ambitious submission with
three half-working features.
Fail honestly
During your Q&A; you may be asked where your
model struggles. Knowing your own limitations is a
strength, not a weakness.
Open data only
All datasets used must be publicly available or
provided by the organisers. Do not use proprietary or
licensed imagery without permission.
